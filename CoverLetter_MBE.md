## Significance and Innovation

This work introduces a fundamentally new approach to Y-chromosome phylogenetic reconstruction based on yin-yang haplotypes (yyhaps)—maximal sets of SNPs exhibiting strict two-state partitioning. Unlike traditional marker-based phylogenetics that requires a priori SNP selection and tree-building algorithms, our framework directly exploits linkage structure in non-recombining regions to infer phylogenetic relationships through hierarchical haplotype nesting.

## Key Findings

Applied to 1,223 males from the 1000 Genomes Project Phase 3, we demonstrate that:

1. **171 yyhaps covering 90.4% of high-quality SNPs** self-organize into a robust phylogenetic tree strongly concordant with established ISOGG haplogroup classifications, validating the approach while revealing fine-scale population structure not previously resolved.

2. **Extreme yyhaps reveal population-specific demographic histories**, including a remarkable 326-SNP block (24 Mb) found exclusively in 20 Tokyo individuals, suggesting a dramatic recent founder event within the past 500 years.

3. **Built-in quality control through phylogenetic consistency checking** identified 23 likely genotyping errors among 9.87 million genotype calls, demonstrating the framework's utility for systematic error detection in large-scale sequencing projects.

4. **Strong support for Out-of-Africa human origins**, with the tree structure recapitulating known migration patterns while providing quantitative evidence for Indo-European expansions, Uralic migrations, and colonial-era gene flow.

## Why MBE?

This manuscript is ideally suited for *Molecular Biology and Evolution* for several reasons:

**Methodological innovation**: The yyhap framework represents a novel computational approach to phylogenetic inference with broad applicability to non-recombining genomic regions and potential extension to recombining chromosomes.

**Evolutionary insights**: Our findings illuminate human demographic history, including founder effects, population expansions, and migration patterns across global populations.

**Community resource**: We provide fully open-source implementation (https://github.com/svanteE/yyhaptree) with complete data and analysis code, facilitating community adoption and extension.

**Methods and Resources focus**: The manuscript emphasizes methodological development and validation, provides detailed algorithmic descriptions, and demonstrates practical applications to forensic genetics, genetic genealogy, and ancient DNA studies.
